Dhodap Fort

About Dhodap Fort

Dhodap Fort, located in the Nashik district, is the second-highest fort in Maharashtra, standing majestically at an elevation of 4,829 feet. This ancient fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding Western Ghats, making it a popular destination for trekkers and nature enthusiasts. Its historical significance is intertwined with the legends of the Maratha Empire, and it is believed to have served as a watchtower for monitoring enemy movements.

The fort is renowned for its impressive architecture, featuring rock-cut steps that lead to its summit. As you trek up these steep and rugged steps, you will encounter remnants of ancient temples, walls, and bastions that speak volumes about its historical importance. The journey is not just a test of endurance but also a chance to immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of Maharashtra.

Upon reaching the top, trekkers are rewarded with panoramic views that extend for miles, showcasing the lush greenery of the valleys below and the rugged peaks of the surrounding hills. The fort's historical narrative is rich with tales of battles and sieges, adding a layer of intrigue to your visit. Dhodap Fort promises an unforgettable experience, merging adventure with a deep appreciation for Maharashtra’s storied past and natural beauty, making it a must-visit for any adventure seeker.

How to Reach

To get to the Dhodap Fort Trek, start at Hatti, a small village in the Nashik district of Maharashtra state. Hatti village is around 60 km far from Nashik.If you’re preparing to drive to Dhodap Fort from Mumbai, you can utilize this link to browse through Google Maps. From Mumbai, take the AH 43, referred to as the Mumbai-Agra highway, and head towards Nashik. When you reach Nashik, continue towards Pimpalgaon Baswant city. From there, take a left turn at Vadalibhoi and continue the highway. Your following location will be Dhodambe, and Hatti village lies around 15 km from Vadalibhoi.
